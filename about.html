Plaridel, Bulacan

Gateway to Progress, Cradle of History

Welcome to Plaridel

Plaridel, originally named Quiñginá, is a first-class municipality in the province of Bulacan, Philippines. Located just 35 kilometers north of Manila, this progressive town serves as an important gateway between Metro Manila and northern Luzon.

With a population of over 100,000 people, Plaridel balances rapid economic development with rich cultural preservation. The town is known for its thriving agriculture, growing industries, and warm, welcoming community.

The town was renamed in honor of Marcelo H. del Pilar, the great Philippine reformist and journalist who was born here on August 30, 1850.

Historical Journey

Early Settlement

Plaridel traces its origins to pre-colonial times when it was called Quiñginá, derived from the Tagalog words "quing" (to block) and "ina" (mother), referring to the town's strategic position controlling river access.

Birthplace of a Hero

In 1850, Marcelo H. del Pilar was born in Barangay San Nicolas. Known as "Bulacan Socrates," he became one of the leading figures of the Propaganda Movement that paved the way for Philippine independence.

The municipality was renamed Plaridel in 1939 under Executive Order No. 158 signed by President Manuel L. Quezon, honoring the town's most illustrious son who fought for press freedom and national sovereignty.

Progress and Development

Economic Growth

Plaridel has evolved into an important economic hub with agriculture, manufacturing, and service sectors contributing to its growth. The town is home to several industrial estates and commercial establishments.

The fertile plains surrounding Plaridel produce rice, corn, and vegetables, while the town's strategic location along major transportation routes has attracted logistics and distribution centers.

Transportation Hub

Plaridel serves as a vital transportation node with the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) Plaridel Bypass connecting major cities and municipalities in Central Luzon to Metro Manila.

The town's excellent transport links have made it a preferred location for businesses and commuters alike, while maintaining its small-town charm and heritage.

Must-See Attractions

Marcelo H. del Pilar Shrine

The ancestral house of Marcelo H. del Pilar, this national shrine preserves the legacy of the revolutionary writer with artifacts and memorabilia from his life and works.

Baras Church

Officially named Sta. Monica Parish Church, this Spanish-era church built in 1850 features unique architectural elements including exterior buttresses and earthquake-resistant design.

Salubong Festival

The Salubong Festival is one of Plaridel’s most anticipated annual events. Featuring a grand parade of decorated horses and carriages, this tradition honors St. James the Apostle, the town’s patron saint. Visitors can enjoy cultural dances, lively music, and a showcase of Plaridel’s proud equestrian heritage.
