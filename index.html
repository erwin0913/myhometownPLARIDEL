THE HEART OF BULACAN

Nestled in the vibrant province of Bulacan, Plaridel shines with its rich history, warm community, and thriving culture. Named after the great patriot Marcelo H. del Pilar, this town embodies the spirit of bravery and progress. From its historic church and ancestral houses to its colorful festivals and modern developments, Plaridel offers a unique blend of heritage and innovation. Now’s the time to explore the charm, hospitality, and stories that make Plaridel the true heart of Bulacan!

CELEBRATIONS IN PLARIDEL

The Colorful Salubong Horse Festival

Salubong Festival

The Salubong Festival is one of Plaridel’s most anticipated annual events. Featuring a grand parade of decorated horses and carriages, this tradition honors St. James the Apostle, the town’s patron saint. Visitors can enjoy cultural dances, lively music, and a showcase of Plaridel’s proud equestrian heritage.

PLARIDEL’S HISTORY

Tracing the Roots

Plaridel, formerly known as Quingua, played a significant role during the Philippine Revolution. It was here that the Battle of Quingua took place in 1899, where Filipino forces bravely fought against American troops. Today, Plaridel stands as a thriving municipality, balancing its historical legacy with modern growth, while remaining a proud symbol of courage and resilience.
